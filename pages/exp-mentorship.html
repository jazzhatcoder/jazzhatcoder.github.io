Engineering Mentor

A professional development opportunity providing guidance and support in applied engineering work within a team environment.

Context

The Prototyping Lab is a hands-on engineering space that supports students in transforming ideas into physical prototypes through tools such as 3D printers, modelling software, and fabrication equipment. As a Prototyping Lab Mentor, I work directly with students across a wide range of projects, providing guidance on design decisions, prototyping workflows, and safe, effective use of lab resources.

I was drawn to this role because it sits at the intersection of technical problem-solving, mentorship, and hands-on engineering. It gave me the opportunity to stay closely involved with the prototyping process while helping others bring their ideas to life, which strongly aligned with my interest in design, collaboration, and practical innovation.

Key Responsibilities

  • Guided students through the prototyping process by supporting 3D modelling, slicing, and 3D printing workflows, helping them move from initial concept to functional physical prototype.
  • Provided technical assistance with lab tools, equipment, and fabrication processes while promoting safe and effective use of prototyping resources.
  • Troubleshot design and manufacturing issues in student projects, offering practical solutions to improve print quality, part functionality, and overall design feasibility.
  • Supported a collaborative lab environment by mentoring students with varying levels of experience and helping them build confidence in hands-on engineering and iterative design.

Technical Skills Applied

In this role, I applied technical skills in 3D modelling, additive manufacturing, and design troubleshooting to support students throughout the prototyping process. I regularly used CAD and slicing software to help refine designs for printability, while drawing on engineering fundamentals such as dimensions, tolerances, material considerations, and iterative testing. The role also strengthened my practical understanding of how theoretical design choices translate into real fabrication constraints, especially when balancing functionality, manufacturability, and time.

Professional Development

Working in a professional engineering environment taught me that technical work is only valuable when it is paired with clear communication, adaptability, and support for others. Beyond the classroom, this role showed me how fast-paced and collaborative prototyping can be, where solving problems often requires balancing technical accuracy, time constraints, user needs, and practical decision-making in real time.

The most valuable thing I gained from this mentorship was learning how to turn technical knowledge into practical support for others, while developing a stronger understanding of how real design challenges are solved through iteration, troubleshooting, and hands-on experience.
