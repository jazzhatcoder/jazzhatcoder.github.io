Undergraduate · 2024 – 2029

B.Eng. Space Engineering

An undergraduate engineering programme combining orbital mechanics, spacecraft systems, robotics, and mission design — building the technical foundation for a career in space systems engineering.

Programme Overview

I chose Space Engineering at York University because it is Canada’s only dedicated Space Engineering program and offers a hands-on, multidisciplinary approach to designing and operating advanced space systems for Earth and beyond. What stood out to me was the program’s emphasis on real satellite hardware, space robotics, flight systems, undergraduate research, and co-op experience, all within Lassonde’s Earth and Space Science and Engineering community.

The program prepares graduates for careers in spacecraft design, satellite communications, robotics, and other high-technology space sectors, while grounding them in accredited engineering training. What makes York’s curriculum especially distinctive from a broader mechanical or aerospace path is its direct focus on the space environment and mission development, including coursework in orbit and attitude dynamics, spacecraft communications, materials for space applications, space hardware, and space mission design using systems engineering methods to balance technical, cost, and programmatic constraints.

  • ESSE 2361 — Space Systems Engineering: introduced me to the systems engineering process, including requirements development, trade studies, subsystem fundamentals, integration, and technical reviews, which directly shaped my interest in mission architecture and interdisciplinary design.
  • ENG 2003 — Effective Engineering Communication: helped me develop technical writing, audience-aware communication, and professional judgment through structured communication assignments grounded in engineering case studies.

Key Academic Projects

One significant academic project was PianoAnalyzer, a MATLAB-based system I developed to detect piano notes in the C4–B4 range through real-time audio analysis. The project involved signal filtering, frequency-domain analysis, note matching, and integration with an Arduino Nano to synchronize LED outputs and button interactions. It strengthened my skills in programming, signal processing, debugging, and hardware-software integration while showing me how analytical tools can be translated into an interactive engineering system.

Another important area of academic work has been my systems-oriented course projects in space engineering, where I applied technical analysis, structured problem-solving, and engineering communication to complex aerospace topics. These projects strengthened my ability to break large technical problems into requirements, trade-offs, and subsystem-level considerations, while also helping me communicate engineering decisions clearly through formal reports, presentations, and design-focused deliverables.

Awards and Recognition

My academic achievements have been recognized through several awards and scholarships, including the Ontario Professional Engineers Foundation for Education Entrance Scholarship, the Ontario Professional Engineers Foundation for Education Undergraduate Scholarship, the BEST (Bergeron Entrepreneurs in Science and Technology) Undergraduate Entrance Award, the Enbridge Engineering Scholarship, and York University Automatic Entrance Scholarships. Together, these awards reflect strong academic performance, leadership potential, and my continued commitment to engineering excellence.
