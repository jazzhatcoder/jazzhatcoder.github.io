Supporting Technical Courses

  • ESSE 4110 — Dynamics of Space Vehicles: built my understanding of orbital and attitude dynamics, spacecraft motion, and control concepts that connect closely to my interests in space robotics and on-orbit operations.
  • ESSE 3360 — Heat Transfer and Thermal Design: strengthened my understanding of thermal behaviour, heat transfer, and engineering design constraints that are critical in spacecraft and hardware systems.
  • ESSE 4350 — Space Hardware: introduced practical approaches to working with space-related hardware, signal acquisition, testing, and system implementation beyond purely theoretical analysis.
  • Applied Mathematics and Physics courses: provided the foundation in differential equations, mechanics, and analytical modelling that I now apply in simulation, rigid body dynamics, and experimental research work.

Notable Academic Projects

One of my most significant academic projects was PianoAnalyzer, a MATLAB-based system I developed to detect piano notes in the C4–B4 range through real-time audio analysis. The project involved filtering recorded signals, extracting dominant frequencies, matching them to note values, and integrating the system with an Arduino Nano to synchronize LED outputs and button interactions. It strengthened my skills in signal processing, debugging, programming, and hardware-software integration while showing me how analytical methods can be translated into an interactive engineering system.

Another important body of academic work has come through my systems and communication courses, where I completed formal technical reports and analytical projects on complex engineering problems. These projects required me to synthesize evidence, break problems into structured sections, justify decisions clearly, and communicate technical ideas to different audiences. Together, they helped me develop the writing, systems thinking, and professional communication skills that underpin the work presented throughout this portfolio.
