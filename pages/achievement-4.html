BEST Entrance Award

An entrance award from the Bergeron Entrepreneurs in Science and Technology program recognizing entrepreneurial initiative, leadership, and enterprising activity demonstrated before university.

Context

The BEST Entrance Award is issued through York Universityâ€™s Bergeron Entrepreneurs in Science and Technology (BEST) program within the Lassonde School of Engineering. It is awarded to incoming Lassonde students whose high school experience shows entrepreneurial initiative, leadership pursuits, and meaningful involvement in enterprising activities.

Application Process

What made this recognition meaningful was that it reflected more than academic performance alone. The award aligned with the leadership, initiative, and project-building experiences I had developed before entering university, especially through technical activities where I had to take ownership, create opportunities, and follow through on ideas with real outcomes.

What It Enabled

Receiving the award reduced some of the financial pressure that comes with starting an engineering degree and gave early validation that the way I approached leadership and innovation had value in a university engineering context. It also strengthened my connection to a community that emphasizes entrepreneurship, initiative, and turning ideas into impact.

This award reflects that I entered engineering not only with strong academics, but with a habit of building, leading, and pursuing ideas proactively.

Looking Forward

The BEST Entrance Award fits naturally with my longer-term goals in robotics, systems engineering, and space technology because those fields reward the same qualities it recognizes: initiative, creativity, and the ability to move from ideas to execution. As I continue developing as an engineer, it stands as an early marker of the kind of impact-driven and entrepreneurial approach I want to bring to future technical work.
