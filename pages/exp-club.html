An active technical leadership role in a student-run aerospace design team, responsible for subsystem design, team coordination, and representing the group in external forums.

Organisation Overview

The York University Robotics Society is a student-led engineering organization focused on developing robotics projects that give members practical experience in design, integration, and teamwork. Through collaborative builds and competition-oriented work, the society creates opportunities for students to apply skills in software, electrical, and mechanical systems while contributing to ambitious, hands-on robotics initiatives.

My Role

In the York University Robotics Society, I contribute to robotics projects through software and electrical development, with an emphasis on subsystem integration and technical problem-solving. My role involves working closely with teammates across disciplines to improve functionality, support prototype development, and help translate design concepts into working robotic systems.

  • Worked on software systems for rover operations, including development related to live media streaming and communication architecture.
  • Contributed to electrical integration efforts by supporting control system improvements and refinement of hardware-related subsystems.
  • Collaborated with mechanical, software, and electrical team members to troubleshoot issues, improve subsystem compatibility, and strengthen overall system performance.
  • Supported the progression of student robotics projects through hands-on design iteration, integration work, and competition-focused development.

Technical Projects

One of the main technical projects I have contributed to through the York University Robotics Society has been rover systems development, particularly on the software side. I worked on a media streaming prototype for rover operations using Media over QUIC (MoQ), helping explore how low-latency communication could support live video transmission and more responsive remote operation. This project gave me experience working with robotics software in a systems context, where communication performance, reliability, and integration directly affect how well the overall platform can function in a competition-oriented environment.

I have also contributed to electrical and integration-focused robotics work aimed at improving subsystem performance and precision. This included supporting projects involving ROS2 and CAN-based integration, as well as work related to encoder-equipped assemblies and custom motor controller development to improve robotic arm control. Through these projects, I gained experience collaborating across software, electrical, and mechanical teams while helping refine real robotic systems through iterative design, troubleshooting, and testing.

Leadership and Communication

One of the most valuable parts of this experience has been learning how to communicate clearly in fast-paced, highly technical team settings. Whether discussing software and electrical integration with teammates, troubleshooting subsystem issues, or explaining design decisions during project development, I learned that strong communication is essential to keeping a multidisciplinary robotics team aligned. This role strengthened my ability to translate technical ideas into clear discussions, collaborate under pressure, and contribute to a team environment where progress depends as much on coordination and clarity as it does on technical skill.

This extracurricular involvement has given me hands-on experience with multidisciplinary engineering in a way that coursework alone cannot fully replicate. It has taught me how to work through real integration challenges, collaborate across technical disciplines, and contribute to projects where progress depends on iteration, communication, and shared problem-solving rather than clearly defined classroom solutions.
